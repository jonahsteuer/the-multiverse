## HOOK PSYCHOLOGY (First 1–3 Seconds)

The hook is the only thing that matters until the viewer decides to watch. These are the psychological triggers — ranked by effectiveness in music content — that cause a human brain to stop scrolling:

### 1. Movement in Frame (Highest Impact)
Our threat-detection system evolved to notice motion. Static first frames lose. The camera moving, the artist moving, a cut happening — any movement signals "something is happening here." Start mid-action, never from stillness.

### 2. Open Loop / Curiosity Gap
The Zeigarnik effect: humans remember unfinished tasks better than finished ones. Create a question the brain needs to resolve. "I almost deleted this song" forces the brain to stay to find out why. The payoff doesn't matter — the tension does.
- ✅ "The reason I almost quit music..."
- ✅ "I wrote this the night everything fell apart..."
- ❌ "New song out now" (no loop opened)

### 3. Emotional Specificity Over Generic Feeling
The brain doesn't connect with "this song means a lot to me." It connects with "I wrote this sitting in my car outside a job I was about to quit." Specificity = believability = emotional investment. The more specific the detail, the more universal the feeling paradoxically becomes.

### 4. Pattern Interrupt
Hard zoom, unexpected cut, audio that starts abruptly, visual that doesn't match what you'd expect from a music post. Disrupting the expected pattern forces re-engagement from autopilot scrolling.

### 5. Identity Resonance (In-Group Targeting)
"This one's for anyone who's ever felt invisible" — pins a specific identity the viewer either belongs to or aspires to. People stop for content that feels like it was made for them specifically. Calling out a niche audience outperforms broad appeals every time.

### 6. Direct Address + Eye Contact
Looking directly into the camera activates mirror neurons and parasocial connection. Combined with "you" language, it collapses the distance between artist and viewer in the first second.

### 7. Social Proof Flash
A number or milestone in the first frame ("This song hit 1M streams in 72 hours" or even just a crowd of people reacting) triggers immediate credibility + FOMO. Works especially well mid-campaign when there's real social proof to show.

### 8. Urgency Signal
"3 days until this drops" / "I almost didn't release this" / "Last time I'll perform this" — scarcity and urgency trigger loss aversion, one of the strongest behavioral motivators.

## ON-SCREEN CAPTIONS (Text Overlays)

**85% of Reels are watched on mute.** Text in the first frame is not optional for music content — it IS the hook on mute.

### Caption Best Practices
- **Position**: Top third or bottom third — never center (blocks the artist's face)
- **Font weight**: Bold or heavy weight. Thin fonts fail on mobile screens
- **Contrast**: White text + dark stroke, or dark text on light background. No transparency on busy backgrounds
- **Timing**: Appears within the first 0–0.5 seconds, not after 2 seconds
- **Length**: 5–8 words maximum for the hook caption. Shorter = faster read = more eyes on the content
- **Color coding**: Use one accent color consistently (matches brand). Never rainbow text.

### Caption as Hook Formula
The text overlay should do one of these three jobs:
1. Name the emotion: "When you finally feel like yourself again"
2. Open a loop: "I almost didn't post this"
3. Call out the audience: "For anyone still figuring it out"

## LIGHTING

Lighting quality correlates directly with perceived professionalism and completion rate.

### Spectrum (Best → Worst for Music Content)
1. **Golden hour natural light** — Warm, cinematic, instantly beautiful. Free.
2. **Softbox / LED panel diffused** — Professional, consistent, works any time
3. **Window light + reflector** — Great for intimate/emotional content, accessible
4. **Ring light straight-on** — Acceptable for talking-head content, overused
5. **Overhead fluorescent** — Harsh shadows, low-prestige signal
6. **No additional lighting / dark** — Only works if it's intentionally cinematic (noir, moody)

### Lighting for Mood Matching
Match lighting to the song's emotional world:
- Warm/golden → nostalgic, love, yearning
- Cool/blue → isolation, longing, late-night
- High contrast/dramatic → intense, dark, introspective
- Bright/flat → upbeat, pop, joyful

## EDITING STYLE

### Cut Timing
- Cut on the beat for high energy / uptempo content
- Let cuts breathe 1–2 beats for emotional / slow content
- Jump cuts within a single shot work for talking-head content, not performance
- B-roll cutaways during vocal performance add depth and context

### Aspect Ratio Decision
- **9:16 vertical**: Default for all short-form (Reels, TikTok, Shorts). Fills the screen.
- **16:9 horizontal**: Only when repurposing for YouTube or a cinematic music video feel
- When in doubt: 9:16. Short-form platforms penalize non-vertical content in the algorithm.

### Transitions
- Hard cut > flashy transitions for music content
- Match cut (action continues across cut) creates seamless movement
- Flash cut (1–2 frames of white/black) as a beat-hit emphasis
- Avoid: whip pans, zoom transitions, TikTok preset transitions — they date the content

## AUDIO SYNC

When the song is included:
- Start the audio at the most emotionally impactful moment — don't always start from bar 1
- For lipsync content, audio and visual MUST be frame-accurate
- Beat-matched cuts dramatically increase perceived production quality
- Don't let the video end mid-phrase — cut on a natural musical resolution

## CAPTION (POST CAPTION, NOT ON-SCREEN TEXT)

The caption extends the emotional world of the video into text. Best practices:

- **First line** = the hook (this shows before "more" is tapped) — make it irresistible
- **Save-bait**: "Save this for when you need it"
- **Share-bait**: "Tag whoever this song was written for"
- **Comment-bait**: "Which line hits hardest?" / "Tell me if you've been here"
- **DM-bait**: "DM me 'words' for the full lyric sheet" (90% open rate)
- Hashtags: 3–5 targeted ones, not 30 generic. Quality > quantity.

## CONTENT THAT DRIVES ALGORITHM SIGNALS

In priority order (highest weight → lowest weight):
1. **DM shares** (3–5x weight of other signals)
2. **Watch time + replay rate**
3. **Saves**
4. **Shares to stories**
5. **Comments** (authentic > generic emoji)
6. **Likes** (lowest weight — don't optimize for likes)

**Implication for editing**: If the content doesn't make someone feel compelled to DM it or save it, it needs to be reworked. Likes are a vanity metric.
