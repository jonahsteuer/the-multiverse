## CURRENT FORMAT PERFORMANCE (Q1 2026)

### What's Overperforming
- **Distraction-format hooks** (unrelated visual → transition to performance) are significantly outperforming pure performance openers for indie/mid-tier accounts. The pattern interrupt is working harder than ever as feeds get more saturated.
- **45-60s story-driven content** is the sweet spot for music artists right now. Shorter pure clips (<20s) are getting watch completion but fewer saves/shares. Longer formats (60s+) need strong narrative payoff to retain — if there isn't a clear arc, it drags.
- **B-Roll + on-screen text** with minimal editing is outperforming highly produced content. The rawness signal is at a premium. iPhone + basic CapCut text is converting better than color-graded + complex transitions for accounts under 100K.

### What's Underperforming
- **Pure performance videos with no text context** are declining in reach for accounts under 50K. Without an identity hook in the first 3 seconds, new viewers have no reason to stay.
- **"Link in bio" CTAs** in captions are actively hurting engagement. Comment-driving CTAs ("drop a 🔥 if you want the full song") outperform external link prompts.
- **Long captions (>80 words)** are being skipped. 10-25 words + 1-2 emojis is the current sweet spot.

## CAPTION STRATEGY (CURRENT)

**What's driving comments right now:**
- Question at the end ("what does this song remind you of?") — drives 2-3x comment rate
- Tease without naming ("can't stop listening to this one" — no song title) — drives "what's the song?" comments which boost algorithmic reach
- First line = hook, rest = optional detail. Instagram cuts captions at ~125 chars before "more" — first line must stand alone.

**What's driving saves:**
- Lyric quotes — fragments that are poetic, specific, or universal tend to trigger "I need to save this"
- "More of this?" CTAs — asking explicitly for saves performs better than no CTA on content where saves would be natural (production tips, origin stories)

## ALGORITHM SIGNALS (RANKED BY CURRENT WEIGHT)

Based on recent content performance data — this ranking shifts; treat as current best estimate:

1. **DM shares** — highest signal. Content people share privately to a specific person is the top reach signal.
2. **Watch time + completion rate** — second most important. A 15s video with 95% completion beats a 60s video with 40%.
3. **Saves** — high signal, especially for discovery. Save = "I want to come back to this."
4. **Comments** — mid signal. More valuable than likes but less than saves. Comment quality matters — "🔥" has less weight than actual text.
5. **Shares to stories** — platform treats this as a strong endorsement signal
6. **Likes** — lowest signal among active engagements. Not unimportant, but weakest.

## FORMAT NOTES FOR MUSIC SPECIFICALLY

- **Performance + text overlay** is still the workhorse format for music artists. Don't abandon it — improve it.
- **Aesthetics format** (b-roll + lyrics) is having a moment for artists with strong lyrical content. Works best when the b-roll is thematic, not random.
- **Skits** are high risk / high reward. When they land, they're the most shareable format. When they don't, they tank completion rate badly. Best for artists with established voice and audience.
