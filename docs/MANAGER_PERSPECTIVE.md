## How The Multiverse Acts as a Manager

### Proactive, Not Reactive

**‚ùå Bad (Reactive):**
- "Here's a calendar. You figure out when to post."
- "Here's some content. You decide what to do with it."
- "Here's some data. You analyze it yourself."

**‚úÖ Good (Proactive - Manager Style):**
- "I've scheduled your posts for the next 8 weeks. Here's your calendar."
- "I've analyzed your performance. This snapshot drove 500 streams. Create 3 more like this."
- "You haven't posted in 3 days. Here's a reminder to post your next snapshot."
- "Your best-performing posts are at 2pm on Tuesdays. I've adjusted your schedule accordingly."

### Always Working

A manager works even when the artist isn't around. The Multiverse should:

- **Send reminders** (email, push notifications)
- **Track performance** (background sync with social APIs)
- **Optimize schedules** (adjust posting times based on performance)
- **Alert on opportunities** ("This snapshot is performing 3x better than average!")
- **Suggest improvements** ("Posts with 80s aesthetic at 2pm reach 3x more new fans")

### Performance-Focused

A manager cares about results, not just activity. The Multiverse should:

- **Track what builds fanbase** (not just likes/views)
- **Show stream attribution** ("This snapshot ‚Üí 500 streams")
- **Identify superfan creation** (repeated engagement, shares, saves)
- **Focus on NEW fans** (not just existing followers)
- **Optimize for growth** (fanbase growth over time)

### Team Coordination

A manager coordinates teams. The Multiverse should:

- **Automatically deliver treatments** (1 week before shoot days)
- **Coordinate availability** (find common available dates)
- **Streamline workflows** (edit upload, review, approval)
- **Keep everyone informed** (activity feed, notifications)
